Mirage

Mirage is an abstract generative art project about facing uncertainty.


Prequel

The seed of Mirage was planted in 2021 with another series which looked nothing like it. Fast forward to 2022, against the post-pandemic and pre-recession backdrop, I felt the need to express the mixed sentiments of anxiety, relief, precariousness and liveliness.

This project was conceived when I newly recovered from COVID and had the urge to create. Then it evolved, along with my perspectives.


Technique

I took the earlier series as a base, and started by projecting it from Cartesian to Polar space.

Moving between spaces offers a transformation of perception. Together they construct parallel versions of reality, different takes of the same situation.

Then I zoomed into the details. Besides changing the base shapes from rectilinear to curvilinear, I also viewed the visual elements of the project as an organic body.


Process

Generative art can be viewed as a living organism consisting of bones, flesh and blood. When constructing generative systems, I first build a grid. This forms the backbone of the artwork, and holds the base shape in place - and they are the flesh. After creating the base shapes, I inspect the recombined elements in the feature space.

I test color palettes and apply those that work best with the specific shapes. I also compose the colors using positional mapping.

Looking back at the evolution of the palettes throughout the project, I notice that I started gloomier. But as optimism gradually took root, the newer palettes started to shift in tonality and carried more sense of agency.
