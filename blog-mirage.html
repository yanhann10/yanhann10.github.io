Mirage



Prequel

The seed of Mirage was planted in 2021 with the Catabolism serie which looks nothing like it. But it didn't germinate back then.

Fast forward to 2022, against the post-pandemic and pre-recession backdrop, I felt the need to convey the mixed sentiments of anxiety, relief, precariousness and liveliness.

This project was conceived when I newly recovered from COVID. There was an urge to create.


Technique

I took the earlier series as a basis, projected it from Cartesian to Polar space.

The thought process behind moving between spaces is that it offers a different perspective/representation that distorts the other space. Together they construct parallel versions of reality - different renditions of perception. And I can envision viewers tunneling between the spaces.

But it goes beyond simply shifting perspectives. The devils are in the details. Besides changing the base shapes from rectilinear to curvilinear, I also viewed every visual element of the project as an organic body.


Viepoint

I view generative art as a living organism consisting of bones, flesh and blood. When constructing this generative system, I first build a grid. This forms the backbone of the artwork, and holds the base shape in place - and they are the flesh. After creating the base shapes, I inspect the recomined elements in the feature space.

I then applied a distinct set of color palettes which are tested to work best with the shapes, and added some positional mapping to the colors.

Looking back at the evolution of the palette throughout the project, I can't help but notice that I started off with gloomier greyer colors, but as optimism gradually took hold of me, the newer palettes started to shift in tonality and carried more sense of agency. Therefore, the duo-toned outputs with both black-and-white and color in one image are among my favorites as they capture the journey of a reborn.



Behind the scene tangents

What's special about this project is that the system was so flexible that it has led me on many tangents, each interesting in its own right. I also stumbled upon several new visuals previously not envisioned.

I tried a few other things and decided to stash those, because they look so serendipitously different. They lean towards math art, and perhaps could be reserved for another day for another audience.
