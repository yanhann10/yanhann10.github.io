Totem of Taste


Totem of Taste is my first generative art project about donuts and is very abstract.

I wanted to explore the monumental presence of what we eat.I think there is something magical about flavors. I'm also often drawn to large public sculptures of food utensils and food themselves, such as the Spoon in Mineapolis, the Fork in Vevey Switzerland or simply those big fallen apples outside De Young in San Francisco.

In constructing the donut shape, I used strange attractors, which are dynamic systems that evolve through time. The algorithm guides the trajectories of millions of points until they settle into deterministic shapes and naturally-formed textures.

I recall the sheer delight of the first sight after stacking together some of those in p5.js, and adore the ethereal glow emanating from the screen.

Here goes some of my favorite output.



While the first version took less than an hour of coding on a genuary prompt, the eventual version was a labor of love that lasted 8 months. Day after day I put in refinements after refinements, which include anything from palette selection to compositional variations, and down to the tinest details such as calibrating the brightness and ensuring visual primitives such as lines and dots are to scale with the screen height.
